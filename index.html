Every year in the bitter cold of the Scranton region thousands go unprepared for the coming winter. While basic needs such as food or a roof over their head are often covered, clothes warm enough to help in the sub freezing temperature are hard to come by. At Elevate the Electric city we aim to provide like new clothing to the disadvantaged of our community, providing them with a sense of both dignity and warmth through the winter months.

Why we do it..


From December to February the average temperature in Scranton is below freezing. The cold, paired with dangerous blizzards and ice storms can create an environment that is deadly and inhospitable. The recent deindustrialization of the North Eastern Pennsylvania region has left many in Scranton unable to afford the costs of heating and warm clothing.

There are many other good groups in the region that try to fill the need of underprivileged Scrantonites, yet we differ in that we try and also provide dignity with the clothing we provide. Our clothing drives focus on gathering new and gently used clothing, and our cash donations go to buying winter clothing out of season in bulk, providing a huge discount. The clothing we provide allows people to walk to jobs openings in a blizzard, and present as a respectable member of society rather than a vagrant. By providing a respectable image, and raising self esteem, we are able to elevate those on the streets to a higher place of being.

Elevate with us!


Elevating the Electric City was founded by local Scrantonites to help and try revitalizing the community for providing for a need we have not seen met by our local charities.

Our goal and mission at Elevating the Electric City is to make sure that no resident of Scranton does not have proper clothing for winter. Through clothing drives, donations from businesses, and cash donations we strive to meet our goals.

The support we have received from our fellow Scranonites and from local businesses has amazed us at every turn. Yet every year we need more clothing, no matter how much we were able to provide last year.

Storage space

In the spring and summer months we easily accumulate stockpiles of winter clothing through various donates and sales. Yet every year we run out of storage space for the clothing we gather, and are forced to rent storage lockers until winter. If you can provide us with any space, in your house, place of work, or property we will be able to spend more money on achieving our goals.

Any space that can be used for clothing drives and distribution is also greatly valued! Warm places where we can distribute our clothing to the less privileged allows them to properly find clothes that fit without being rushed in the middle of a blizzard, and ensures a much smoother process and a larger turn out.

Volunteers

The donations we receive in our clothing drives always astounds us, yet we do not have the manpower to organize nearly as many as we would like. With more volunteers we will be able to spread further out, and service more areas.

  • We need volunteers for:
    • Clothing drives
    • Securing donations
    • Maintaining relations with our various partners around the area
    • Inventorying our clothing stocks
    • Mending donations not in the best of shape
Clothing

Our most pressing need and the core of our mission is acquiring clothing to give to our disadvantaged. Our donations turn up coats, sweaters and jackets in piles, but every year we lack the amount of socks, gloves, and hats we need.

  • We will not turn away any donation of clothing, but what we desperately need for next winter is:
    • Socks (preferably wool)
    • Hats
    • Gloves
    • Thank the interviewer.
  • Donations of anything else that will help will be gladly accepted. Blankets, portable heaters, cloth, thread, and needles are all incredibly useful in making sure no one in Scranton goes to bed cold.
